Welcome to Elasticedit

Whether you are building solutions for search and data use cases like Observability, Security and Enterprise Search, or building for your organization’s unique needs, Elastic has something to amplify your effort and propel your organization to new, valuable places. Search, observe, protect and much more.

This guide will take you through your first steps.

Get ready to create!

If you are reading this, we expect that you are new to Elastic and are starting with Elastic Cloud. If you want to host things yourself and take a more advanced path, consider Getting Started with the Elastic Stack.

Before you beginedit

We will create your first Elastic Cloud deployment. Consider this deployment your proof of concept or "first tour" into what Elastic has to offer before you get deep into production deployments.

What you will need:

  • An email account
  • An hour
  • Data — from websites, apps, services and more.

Sign up for Elastic Cloudedit

First, start a Cloud trial.

Next, give your first Elastic deployment a name.

As your deployment materializes, you’ll receive a super-user login.

Keep it secret. Keep it safe. It provides full access to your deployment.

It is the key to your castle.

Click Continue when you are ready.

Finally, choose from our growing integrations library and add your data.

Wait, but why? What is happening?edit

As soon as you create your first deployment and enter it, you are within Kibana. Kibana is the window into Elastic and all the use cases it supports. It is how you will connect to the Elastic Stack powering your new deployment.

Under the hood, Kibana connects to Elasticsearch, the fast, scaleable and relevant search engine; the heart of the Elastic Stack. Elasticsearch is known for well, you know, search. Search and its rapid retrieval of "the right information" can catalyze many powerful use cases — at scale.

The only thing you need to start playing is data.

Add your dataedit

Your organization generates data - lots of it.

They might be in the form of metrics, logs, objects, keys and values. And they might come from your servers, your in-house apps, your web pages, your third party tools or from your customers. Wherever they are coming from, you will want to "ingest" them — index them into Elasticsearch — and then build on them. The more, different types of data you ingest, the more powerful the insights will be to inform key decisions and drive growth.

From the Integrations experience, select the applications your team is currently using.

This flow introduces two integration types: Beats and Elastic Agents.

Beats have been relied upon at scale as lightweight data shippers, sending logs, metrics and uptime information into Elasticsearch. There is a robust library from which to choose.

Elastic Agents are newer and they get deeper, sending logs, metrics and security data, while also monitoring for and preventing security threats. More are becoming available all the time.

Choose among the list, using search if you need to look for a specific integration. If you are looking to integrate another Elastic product, consider one of the following options:

  • If you want to begin using Elastic to observe and protect your infrastructure, consider installing Elastic Endpoint onto your workstations.
  • If you want to gain deeper insights into your service and application behaviour, check out Elastic APM.
  • If you have websites or applications that you want to crawl as a first step in providing search, check out the Elastic App Search web crawler.
  • If you want to use a programming language like JavaScript, Java, Python, Ruby, and more to index data into Elasticsearch, checkout the Elasticsearch language clients.

Find the integrations you need, install them and bring all of your data together into one place.

Yes! And what now?edit

Congratulations! You have added data into Elastic.

Getting your data in and ready to explore is the first, big step.

Now to get deeper.

Elastic offers leading solutions for:

  • Exploring your data
  • Observability
  • Elastic Security
  • Enterprise Search
  • Machine Learning

And many other tools to build your own fast, scaleable, and insightful data applications.

If you are unsure where to go, consider one of the following:

  1. Add search to your website with Elastic App Search
  2. Create an internal search portal with Elastic Workplace Search
  3. Visualize your data with Discover in Kibana
  4. Secure your infrastructure with Elastic Endpoint Security
  5. Observe your systems with Elastic APM
  6. Setup secure access in Kibana for your team
  7. Return to the add your data page and add more integrations!
